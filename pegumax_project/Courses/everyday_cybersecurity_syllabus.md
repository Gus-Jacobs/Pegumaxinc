# Everyday Cybersecurity & Digital Privacy

*A hands-on course for normal people who want to lock down their digital lives. No degree required—just practical defenses you can set up this weekend.*

---

## Module 1: The Threat Landscape & Your Personal Attack Surface
**Understand who's actually after your data and where you're realistically exposed, so you can prioritize defenses that matter.**

- Mapping your attack surface: accounts, devices, networks, and data trails
- Real threats vs. movie threats: phishing, credential stuffing, SIM swaps, and data breaches
- Building a personal threat model so you protect the right things first

---

## Module 2: Password Architecture & Management
**Design a password system that's both unbreakable and effortless to use across hundreds of accounts.**

- Why length beats complexity: entropy, passphrases, and how passwords actually get cracked
- Choosing and configuring a password manager (vault structure, autofill, secure sharing)
- Eliminating reuse: auditing breached credentials and rotating high-value logins

---

## Module 3: Credential Hardening & Multi-Factor Authentication
**Add layers beyond the password so a leaked credential alone never gets anyone into your accounts.**

- MFA tiers compared: SMS vs. authenticator apps (TOTP) vs. hardware keys (FIDO2/passkeys)
- Securing account recovery: backup codes, recovery emails, and closing the reset loophole
- Passkeys in practice—going passwordless on the services that support it

---

## Module 4: Securing Your Devices
**Lock down the phones and computers that hold the keys to everything else.**

- Full-disk encryption, screen locks, and biometric tradeoffs on desktop and mobile
- App permissions, sideloading risks, and trimming background data access
- Update hygiene, secure boot, and what to do when a device is lost or stolen

---

## Module 5: Home Network Defense
**Turn your home network from a soft target into a hardened first line of defense.**

- Router hardening: firmware updates, strong admin credentials, and disabling risky features (WPS, UPnP)
- Wi-Fi security: WPA3, guest networks, and isolating smart-home (IoT) devices
- Network segmentation and a simple firewall mindset for the home

---

## Module 6: Network Traffic Inspection with Wireshark
**See exactly what your devices are sending over the network so hidden data leaks have nowhere to hide.**

- Capturing and reading packets: filters, protocols, and following a TCP stream
- Spotting unencrypted traffic, suspicious connections, and chatty apps phoning home
- Identifying who your apps talk to and confirming whether sensitive data is encrypted in transit

---

## Module 7: DNS-Level Ad Blocking & Tracker Control
**Block ads, trackers, and malicious domains for every device at once—before they ever load.**

- How DNS works and why filtering at this layer beats per-browser blockers
- Setting up Pi-hole or a private DNS service (DNS-over-HTTPS/TLS) for whole-network protection
- Building and tuning blocklists without breaking the sites you actually use

---

## Module 8: Private & Secure Browsing
**Reclaim your browsing from the trackers and fingerprinting that follow you across the web.**

- Browser hardening: extensions, container tabs, and anti-fingerprinting settings
- Cookies, tracking pixels, and how cross-site profiles are built (and broken)
- When and how to use a VPN or Tor—real benefits vs. marketing myths

---

## Module 9: Encrypted Communication & Data Privacy
**Keep your messages, files, and backups readable only by the people you choose.**

- End-to-end encrypted messaging and email: what's protected and what metadata leaks
- Encrypting files and backups locally and in the cloud
- Data minimization: shrinking your footprint with opt-outs, deletions, and burner identities

---

## Module 10: Incident Response & Staying Secure
**Know exactly what to do when something goes wrong, and build habits that keep you safe long-term.**

- Responding to a breach: containment, password resets, and freezing your credit
- Recognizing active compromise—unusual logins, account lockouts, and recovery steps
- A sustainable security routine: periodic audits, breach monitoring, and staying current
